Welcome to My Portfolio

About

Hello! My name is Adam, and I am passionate about leveraging data to drive impactful change. I majored in Physics from the University of Washington, where I developed a strong foundation in analytical thinking and problem-solving. My journey into the world of data began through my experiences in creating and managing healthcare startups. I have been instrumental in the establishment of four startups, focusing on mental health and eye clinics. These ventures fueled my interest in data analysis as I sought to optimize operations and improve patient outcomes.

In addition to my entrepreneurial endeavors, I am deeply committed to making a difference in the community. I founded a non-profit organization dedicated to supporting individuals with ocular melanoma, where I currently serve as the president. I also contribute as the treasurer for another non-profit aimed at assisting those with autism. My work in these non-profits has honed my skills in leadership, financial management, and strategic planning.

My curiosity and passion for data analysis naturally progressed into an interest in data science and machine learning. I believe that machine learning is an extraordinary tool with vast potential across various domains, including business and science. It enables us to uncover patterns, make predictions, and automate decision-making processes in ways that were previously unimaginable.

Outside of my professional life, I enjoy a variety of hobbies that keep me active and engaged. I am an avid weightlifter, runner, and cyclist, constantly pushing my physical limits. In the kitchen, I find joy in cooking and baking, experimenting with new recipes. When I seek relaxation, I turn to reading and photography, capturing moments and stories through my lens. These diverse interests not only keep me balanced but also fuel my creativity and problem-solving skills.

Projects

Customer Segmentation with K-Means and Customer Recommender System

Implemented K-Means clustering to segment customers and developed a recommender system to suggest products tailored to each segment, enhancing personalization and customer satisfaction as well as increased sales.
A/B Testing

Conducted A/B testing on customer conversion rates using Pearson's chi-square and Fisher's exact tests to evaluate statistical significance. Analyzed sample size, metrics, and sampling distributions to optimize test accuracy and ensure valid conclusions.
Customer Churn and Profit Analysis

This project analyzes customer churn data to identify key factors driving churn and assesses the impact on profitability, using predictive modeling techniques.
WA State Earthquake Prediction Project

A comprehensive analysis of earthquake data specific to Washington State that identifies cities most affected by earthquakes, plot historical earthquakes along fault lines, and use machine learning to make predictions of future earthquake probabilities.
Illegal Fisheries Prediction Project

This project uses data science and machine learning techniques to unravel patterns of illegal fishing by analyzing transshipment and loitering events, vessel encounters and identifies hotspots of such activities, and understand the spatial and temporal dynamics of these vessels.
Camouflaged Tank Detection Project

Detect camouflaged vehicles in various terrains using image processing techniques and subsequently track their locations using geospatial data. Involves enhancing image visibility, identifying objects, and determining their geographical coordinates.
Biodiversity In Our National Parks

Comprehensive analysis of biodiversity data in national parks. Using exploratory data analysis (EDA) to understand the distribution of species and their conservation status. Using machine learning models to predict conservation needs and to provide insights into the biodiversity of national parks.
Money and Tree Coverage Correlation in NYC

An analysis of the financial investments and distribution of trees across New York City. This dashboard also highlights potential disparities and trends in urban forestry investments, offering a clear overview of how financial resources impact environmental planning in the city.
Book Recommender Program

Using GoodReads dataset, we perform exploritory data analysis to asses various aspects of the dataset. We then use K-Nearest Neighbors to assemble a book recomender system where we can search for a book and then see what books are similar to it based upon reviews.
GDP-to-Life Expectancy Correlation

Explores the relationship between GDP and life expectancy across different countries. The analysis uses statistical methods to identify patterns and assess the impact of GDP on life expectancy, and socioeconomic factors influencing health outcomes.
NYC Housing Pricing Project

Using exploratory data analysis (EDA) and XGBoost for predictive modeling. The notebook breaks down the factors influencing housing prices to better understand housing costs in NYC. The notebook then builds and evaluates an XGBoost model to predict housing cost with hypertuning.
Wildlife Cost to the Airline Industry

Visualizes data on the frequency and impact of wildlife strikes on aircraft in the US. The dashboard offers insights into geographic hotspots for these incidents and the associated economic costs, helping to understand the scope of the issue and inform mitigation strategies.
Wine Quality Prediction

Aanalysis of wine quality using exploratory data analysis (EDA) and machine learning. Involves performing EDA to understand the relationships between different features and wine quality, and applying various machine learning models to predict the quality of wine.
Bank Churn Rate

Analyzing patterns and correlations related to bank customer churn, and building an XGBoost model to predict churn rates. The notebook includes feature engineering, model training, and evaluation using metrics like accuracy, precision, recall, and the ROC-AUC score to assess the model's performance.
Telescope Data Analysis using PCA

Demonstrates the use of Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to analyze data collected from telescopes. PCA helps to improve computational efficiency by reducing the number of dimensions while retaining important information.
